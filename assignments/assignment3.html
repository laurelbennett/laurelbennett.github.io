Salami Basics

Salami is a popular cured pork sausage. Some of the most popular include

Genoa and Pepperoni. Salami goes great on sandwiches, with charcuterie boards,

and just plain. The word originates from the Italian word, "salame."

Salami can also be made from beef, or veal.

Origins

Salami originates from Italy. It became popular among European peasants

because it can be stored at room temperature for up to 40 days. Salami is fermented,

which means that it is produced with the help of microorganisms. Some ingredients

typically used in the Salami making process include garlic and salt.

Types

Various types of Salami exist. Some examples include:

  • Genovese
  • Pepperoni
  • Chorizo
  • Hard Salami
  • Felino
